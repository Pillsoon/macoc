What Is This Document?

We are proposing a new website system for MACOC. Before we finalize anything, we would like to share the plan and get your feedback.

The current website uses Wix for the website and Jotform for registration forms. These services have served us well, but we believe there is an opportunity to simplify management and reduce costs by moving to a new setup.

This document outlines the proposed plan in three areas:

  1. Website Content — How content would be managed going forward
  2. Registration Form — How the registration and data tracking would work
  3. Billing — How payments would be processed

Nothing is final yet. We welcome your questions, suggestions, and feedback so we can build the best solution for everyone.

2. Registration Form Management

How would the registration process work?

The proposed registration form would guide students through 3 simple steps:

Step 1: Student & Teacher Information

  • Teacher: Name, email, phone number
  • Student: Name, email, date of birth, age, address
  • Proof of age upload (birth certificate or passport)

Step 2: Repertoire Information

  • Select division (e.g., Piano)
  • Select section (based on age)
  • Two pieces: Title, composer, time period

Step 3: Review, Submit & Pay

  • Review all entered information
  • Click the Submit button
  • Complete payment on the Stripe payment page
  • Automatic confirmation email sent to student/parent after successful registration and payment

Where would you view registrations?

Directly in Google Sheets.

Every time someone registers, a new row would be automatically added to a Google Sheets spreadsheet. For example:

  • Who registered (student name, teacher name)
  • When they registered (date and time)
  • Whether they paid (Payment Status: Pending = not yet, Paid = done)
  • Which division (Piano, Strings, etc.)
  • What pieces they will perform
  • Proof of age document (click the link to view it instantly)
Key Point: Instead of logging into a separate service, all registration data would be available in Google Sheets — a tool many of us already use.

Where would proof-of-age documents be stored?

Documents uploaded by students (birth certificates, passports) would be stored securely in Cloudinary, a safe file storage service.
You would click the link in Google Sheets to view any document instantly.

3. Billing Management

How would the payment process work?

1. Student fills out the registration form and submits it

2. A "Complete Payment" button appears

3. Clicking the button takes them to a Stripe payment page

4. Student (or parent) enters their card information and pays

5. Payment is recorded in Google Sheets

6. Confirmation email is sent to the student/parent

What is Stripe?

Stripe is one of the most widely used online payment services in the world.
Major companies like Amazon and Google use it. It is safe and reliable.

Where would you check payment status?

You would be able to check in two places:

  1. Google Sheets — Check the "Payment Status" column in the registrations list
  2. Stripe Dashboard (stripe.com) — View payment amounts, dates, and details

Designated admin(s) would be given edit access to the Google Sheets and the Stripe dashboard. The developer would support initial setup and major changes, but day-to-day updates (dates, fees, winners, etc.) can be handled by chairs without any technical knowledge.

Ownership: All data and accounts — including the domain, Google Sheets, and Stripe — would be owned by MACOC, not by the developer. MACOC retains full control at all times.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I'm not familiar with Google Sheets. Is that okay?

A: If you've ever used Excel, it's almost the same. Just click a cell and type. It saves automatically.

Q: Is student data secure?

A: Yes. Access to Google Sheets would be restricted to authorized MACOC staff only. Uploaded documents (such as proof of age) are stored securely in Cloudinary and are not publicly accessible. Payments are handled entirely by Stripe, and credit card information is never stored on the MACOC website.

Q: What if someone accidentally changes something in Google Sheets?

A: Google Sheets keeps automatic version history, so any accidental changes can be reviewed and rolled back easily.

Q: Is the new website reliable?

A: The website would be hosted on Vercel, a professional hosting platform used by millions of websites worldwide. If there is ever an issue, you can reach out to the developer for support.

Q: What if we want to change the registration form?

A: To change the form fields or questions, please ask the developer. Simple changes can usually be made quickly.

Q: Where does the payment money go?

A: It goes directly to the bank account linked to MACOC's Stripe account. You can check everything in the Stripe Dashboard.

Q: Will all divisions be available (Strings, Voice, etc.)?

A: We plan to add all divisions. The initial launch would start with Piano, and other divisions would follow.

Q: What if the developer is no longer available?

A: All data and accounts — including the domain, Google Sheets, and Stripe — would be owned by MACOC. Day-to-day content updates can be done by anyone with Google Sheets access. If a new developer is ever needed, they can pick up where things left off since all code and data belong to MACOC.
