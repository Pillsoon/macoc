Competition Regulation

Piano

  • Select two pieces representing different periods and styles.
  • All works must be performed by memory in the Divisions.
  • Age cut off date is April 30, 2024.
  • Within reason, works should avoid repeats.
  • No changes to the competition program are permitted once the application form has been received.
  • The decisions of the Jury are final and not subject to review or appeal.
  • The Jury is permitted to award the same numerical ranking to several contestants. In the event of a tie, the Jury may divide the monetary prize equally between the prizewinners.
  • Performer should bring the original copy of repertoires in their performance.

Vocal: Classical

Choose one selection from Group A and Group B* in contrasting style. You may cut the accompaniment to meet the time limit. If it is longer than the suggested time, the judges might cut your performance short to meet the time limit.

  • You could cut the long piano part to fit in the time frame.
  • All original scores or sheet music purchased online and downloaded/printed must be accompanied by a receipt. These are required.
  • Vocal participants must provide their own accompanist. Teachers may not accompany their vocal students.

Section I : Age 8 and under (6 minutes)

A. One English Art song or folk song
B. One English Art song

Section II : Age 9-10 (6 minutes)

A. One English song by a British or American composer
B. Any classical Italian art song from the 17th or 18th Century

Section III : Age 11-12 (8 minutes)

A. One English song by a British or American composer
B. Any classical Italian art song from the 17th or 18th Century

Section IV : Age 13-14 (8 minutes)

A. One classical art song in any language (original language)
B. One English song by a British or American composer

Section V : Age 15-16 (8 minutes)

A. One classical art song in any language (original language)
B. One English song by a British or American composer

Section VI : Age 17-18 (10 minutes)

A. One classical art song in any language
B. (1) An Oratorio Aria or an aria from the standard operatic repertoire in the original language and key. (2) One French art song or lied in the original language.

Section VII : Age 19-22 (10 minutes)

A/B. Any classical repertoire. ( Should include at least one Opera aria in the original key and the language it's written in)

Section VIII : Age 21 and above (12 minutes)

A/B. Any classical repertoire. ( Should include at least one Opera aria in original key and the language it's written in)

Vocal: Musical Theater

Please be prepared with an age-appropriate two contrasting songs from this list of musical theater shows or anything from "The Singer's Musical Theater Anthology," "The Musical Theater Anthology for Teens," "The First Book of Broadway Solos," "The Kids Broadway Book," "The Big Book of Broadway" by Hal Leonard Publishing, or "Broadway Showstoppers" by Hal Leonard Publishing.

Singers may also want to choose works by the following composers: Irving Berlin, Stephen, Flaherty, George Gershwin, Jerry Herman, Frank Loesser, Alan Menken, Cole Porter, Rodgers and Hammerstein, Stephen Schwartz, Charles Strouse, or Jules Styne (you are not limited to these composers).

Again, there is so much new literature coming out regularly that we ask that you use your discretion in choosing repertoire in the older categories. If you have any questions about appropriate repertoire, check with the Chairman.

  • You could cut the long piano part to fit in the time frame.
  • All original scores or sheet music purchased online and downloaded/printed must be accompanied by a receipt. These are required.
  • Vocal participants must provide their own accompanist. Teachers may not accompany their vocal students.

Strings + Piano Chamber

Eligibility

More than 2 and up to 5 members in a group

Strings only: String Duo, String Trio, String Quartet, String Quintet

Strings + Piano: Piano trio, Piano quartet, Piano quintet

RULES

  • Memorization is NOT required.
  • Repertoire must be chosen from the standard literature and should demonstrate excellent musicianship and proper technique.
  • No change in the repertoire is allowed following submission of the application
  • At least ONE Score needs to be given to the judges
  • All members of the group can use an original copy or sheet music from the public domain.
  • Taking pictures or video recording is NOT allowed inside the competition room.
  • Judges decision is FINAL

Guitar: Chamber Music

  • Up to 5 members in the chamber music ensemble
  • Memorization is NOT required.
  • Category is determined by age of the oldest member
  • Each ensemble should present TWO contrasting pieces.
  • Repertoire may be freely chosen and should demonstrate excellent musicianship and proper technique.
  • No changes in repertoire are allowed following submission of the application
  • All members of the group must use original (not photocopied) sheet music

Woodwinds

  • Repertoire must be chosen from the standard literature, and should demonstrate excellent musicianship and proper technique.
  • No cutting or rearranged the music is permitted, except for the piano part.
  • No changes in repertoire are allowed following submission of the application.
  • Each entrant must provide his or her own pianist.
